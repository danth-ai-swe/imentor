Here's another type of promotion: advertising. I think we all know
advertising when we see it, but could you define it?
Advertising is any paid form of nonpersonal communication or promotion
about a company or its products or services that an identified sponsor
generates and transmits through any type of media.
Insurers typically use advertising media such as television, radio, direct mail,
the internet, social media, magazines, newspapers, billboards, and posters.
Advertising media can reach a high number of people but in many cases is
not customized for individuals. The internet can allow for some degree of
customization, however (for example, based on previous purchases or
demographic profile).
Institutional Advertising
One way insurers reach prospective customers is through institutional
advertising, also called image advertising, which seeks to promote a general
image of the company rather than to explain a specific product. For
example, insurers may use institutional advertising to try to convey an
image of strength, trust, reliability, longevity, or stability.
Product Advertising
Insurers also use product advertising to promote a specific product. Complex
insurance products can be difficult to explain adequately in an
advertisement. Product advertising can be aimed at financial professionals
or the end customer.

Sources of Marketing Information
Click or touch each heading below to learn about sources of marketing

Internal Databases
Internal databases contain internal records and reports from throughout
the company. These reports provide detailed information about a company's
sales, expenses, profitability, products, promotions, distribution, customers,
and markets.
Website and Social Media Analysis
Insurers can gain important insight into customer needs, interests, and
attitudes by analyzing data about customer behavior on the company's
website and on social media. Insurers may collect clickstream data to gain
insight into how to best meet customer product needs or improve the
customer experience. Many companies also pay attention to what customers
and others say about the company or its products on social media, blogs,
and online forums.
Competitive and Market Intelligence
Insurers need information, often called competitive intelligence, about their
competitors. Specifically, insurers want to know about their competitors'

===== Trang 40 / 51 =====

marketing mixes, target markets, service quality, market shares, marketing
strategies, and product mixes.
In addition, insurers need information about ongoing developments in the
marketing environment, often called market intelligence. Specifically,
insurers want to identify issues that might impact a market's potential.
Insurers obtain most of their competitive and market information from
publicly available sources, like company websites or financial services
publications.
Marketing Research
Through marketing research, an insurer or independent company collects,
interprets, and reports information related to specific marketing problems
or opportunities. It provides information about customers, products, and
distribution channels on an as-needed or one-time basis.
The Marketing Environment
Insurers analyze marketing information within the context of the company's
current marketing environment. The marketing environment consists of all
of the elements in the company's internal and external environments that
affect the company's ability to carry out its marketing activities.

A company's internal environment consists of elements within the
company's control, such as financial, physical, technological, and
human resources; organizational structure; and the marketing mix.

A company's external environment consists of elements outside the
company's control, such as economic, competitive, regulatory,
taxation, and social factors.
