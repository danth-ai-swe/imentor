Module 2: Serving the Customer throughout the
Policy Lifecycle
A customer's contact with an insurer most likely begins when they are
thinking about purchasing a policy, and can last until the policyowner
dies and the insurer pays the policy proceeds to the beneficiary. In
between, the insurer underwrites and issues the policy, and meets the
customer’s service needs while the policy is in force. In this module, you’ll
learn about the tasks an insurer performs at each stage of the policy
lifecycle.

Engagement Methods
Learning Objective
Describe the various engagement methods insurers and financial

Financial products don't reach the marketplace the way consumer goods do.
Think about it: financial products can't sit in a display window or on a
showroom floor. Without an effective distribution system, these products
won't reach customers where, when, and how they want. That's why
distribution is critical.
Insurers make decisions about which distribution options to use based on the
customers they are trying to serve. From a customer's perspective,
distribution is all about convenience. Although many customers do initial

Direct Mail or Email

===== Trang 8 / 68 =====

An insurer or financial professional using direct mail or email distributes
insurance sales materials through a mail service directly to a list of
prospective customers. These mailings can be physical letters, brochures, or
flyers mailed to the prospect or emails sent to a distribution list. The target
market for direct mail might be readers of a particular publication or
holders of a particular credit card.
For paper mail, the sales materials usually consist of an introduction letter,
a brochure that describes a particular product, an insurance or annuity
application, or an inquiry form the customer can use to request further
information about the product. For email, the insurer provides links to
similar items on the insurer's website.

Print and Broadcast Media

===== Trang 10 / 68 =====

example, a life insurance product might be advertised on television between
the hours of 8 and 10 p.m. when newly married couples or young parents
are likely watching television.

Market Conduct

===== Trang 33 / 68 =====

Insurers must regularly monitor the sales activities of all of their agents to
ensure compliance with market conduct laws. And whenever regulatory
requirements change, insurers are required to communicate the changes to
agents accurately and promptly.
An insurer's monitoring system should include a method for identifying and
reporting agents who are found to be unsuitable to sell insurance products.

additional training. However, serious infractions, or a pattern of minor
infractions, require disciplinary action, up to and including a termination of
the agency contract.

conduct that violates the laws in that jurisdiction. Unlawful sales practices
include churning, twisting, and rebating.

Broker-Dealers

===== Trang 40 / 68 =====

Not to be confused with an insurance broker, a broker-dealer is a financial
institution that buys and sells securities either for itself or for its customers.
Common securities are stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. In the United
States, securities can be distributed only through a broker-dealer that is
registered with the federal Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and is
a member of the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA).
In the United States, variable life insurance and variable annuity products
are considered securities as well as insurance products. To distribute them,
many insurers establish a subsidiary that is registered as a broker-dealer
(known as an insurance broker-dealer).
Alternatively, an insurer can market the products through an unaffiliated
broker-dealer. Broker-dealers range from small companies to huge,
nationally known institutions.

===== Trang 41 / 68 =====

Individuals who work for a broker-dealer selling securities and are
appropriately registered with FINRA are known as registered
representatives, or registered reps. An insurer's wholesalers provide
education and support materials to registered reps regarding the insurer's
products and promote the advantages of the insurer's products compared
to other insurers' products.
Registered Investment Advisers
Registered investment advisers (RIAs) provide advice on investing in
securities. Larger investment adviser firms—those that manage more than
$100 million in client assets—register with the SEC, while smaller
investment adviser firms register with state securities authorities.
Many RIAs charge their clients an annual fee based on the value of all the
investments the adviser manages on the client’s behalf (known as assets

===== Trang 42 / 68 =====

under management, or AUM). Therefore, insurers have created products
that provide for the adviser to be paid a fee that is a percentage of the
account value, rather than to be compensated by commission. Some broker-
dealers also switched in recent years from commissions to fee-based
compensation.
Banks and Other Depository Institutions
In the United States, banks and other depository institutions generally
cannot issue insurance, but they can distribute insurers' products. When
banks offer insurance, a licensed insurance company must underwrite and
accept the risk associated with the insurance products the bank sells. Also,
banks are not permitted to make the purchase of an insurance product a
condition for a customer's obtaining a loan from the bank.

Prohibited Sales Practices
In the United States, insurers must abide by the market conduct laws described earlier
in the lesson, which govern how insurers and financial professionals behave. Market
conduct laws prohibit certain sales practices that can harm customers.

When a financial professional promises a benefit to a prospect to purchase a
policy or annuity when that benefit isn't in the product itself and isn't offered
to all others, he is engaging in rebating.

If a financial professional misrepresents the features of a policy to persuade a
customer to replace his current policy, the financial professional is engaging in
twisting.

A financial professional that convinces a customer to replace policies or annuities
over and over so the financial professional can earn many high commissions is
engaging in churning.
To guard against violations of these laws, insurers develop compliance programs that
teach, train, and monitor employees and financial professionals regarding all market
conduct laws and procedures.
