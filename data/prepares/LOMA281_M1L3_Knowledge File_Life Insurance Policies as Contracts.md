Learning Objective

===== Trang 6 / 26 =====

Contracts come in a variety of types. Every state has a large body of law
(hệthống pháp luật đồsộ) that governs the operation and effect of
contracts (điều chỉnh cách thức hoạt động và hiệu lực của hợp đồng).
The specific rules of contract law that apply to a given contract vary by the
type of contract.
Let’s look at some of the most common types.
Unilateral and Bilateral Contracts
(Hợp đồng đơn phương và hợp đồng song phương)
Able Life can’t make Simone pay her premiums. But as long as she does pay,
Able Life’s promise to pay the policy’s benefit is legally enforceable (có hiệu
lực pháp lý).
Because only one party to the contract makes a promise that can be
enforced in a court of law (được thực thi trước pháp luật), the insurance
contract is a unilateral contract (hợp đồng đơn phương).
In a bilateral contract (hợp đồng song phương), both parties make legally
enforceable promises (cảhai bên đều đưa ra những cam kết có hiệu lực
pháp lý).

Like-for-Like Exchange
A transaction where both parties trade items or services of equal value and
similar nature.
Aleatory and Commutative Contracts
In a commutative contract (hợp đồng đối ứng), the parties agree to
exchange specified items or services that are equal in value.
In an aleatory contract, one party exchanges something of value for the
other party’s conditional promise.
Aleatory Contract
Under an aleatory contract (hợp đồng ngẫu hiên), if the specified event
occurs, one party may then receive something of greater value than that
party gave.

Bargaining Contracts
In a bargaining contract, both parties set the terms and conditions.
Contracts of Adhesion
Unlike a bargaining contract, a contract of adhesion (hợp đồng gia nhập) is
one in which one party sets the contract terms that the other party must
accept or reject outright.
An insurance contract is not a bargaining contract because the policyowner
has no say about the terms of the contract. Able Life prepares the contract
and Simone must accept it or reject it without any bargaining between
them. This makes it a contract of adhesion.

===== Trang 9 / 26 =====

With a contract of adhesion, the courts usually interpret any ambiguities
(tòa án thường giải thích bất kỳsựmơ hồnào) in the contract against the
party that drafted the contract.
For example, if a provision (điều khoản) in Simone’s life insurance policy is
ambiguous, a court would likely interpret the ambiguous policy provision
against Able Life and in favor of Simone, or her beneficiary.
Policy Language in Contracts of Adhesion
Life insurance contracts have easily readable language and problems that
can crop up by having easily readable language.
Informal and Formal Contracts
Informal contract: A contract that is enforceable because the parties to the
contract met requirements concerning (đáp ứng) the substance (nội dung)
of the agreement rather than requirements concerning the form of the
agreement (hình thức của thỏa thuận)
Formal contract: A contract that is enforceable because the parties met
certain formalities concerning the form of the agreement.
A formal contract requires the parties to meet certain formalities, such as
creating the contract using legal counsel or having the contract validated by
a notary public. A real estate sales contract is a formal contract.

Valid, Void, and Voidable Contracts
Whether Simone’s rights under her life insurance policy and Able Life’s
obligations under the policy are enforceable at law depend on whether the
contract was issued according to certain principles of contract law and is a
valid contract.
A contract is valid if it satisfies all legal requirements. A contract that does
not meet one or more of the legal requirements to create a valid contract is
void. A voidable contract is a contract under which one party has the right
to avoid his obligations under the contract. The other party, however, is
bound by the terms of the contract.

obligations under the contract without incurring legal liability.

Legally Adequate Consideration
(Giá trịtrao đổi hợp pháp)
The parties to the contract must exchange consideration (trao đổi giá trị),
which means that each party must give or promise something that is of
value to the other party.
The rules of contract law don’t require the parties to exchange equal
consideration. Whatever is of value to the parties generally is considered to
be legally adequate consideration.
Applicant's Consideration
If the applicant had not paid the initial premium, she would not have
provided consideration (giá trịtrao đổi) and no contract would exist.

Renewal premiums (phí bảo hiểm tái tục) payable to keep the policy in force
are not consideration for the formation of the contract but a condition
necessary for the continuation of the contract.
The applicant’s consideration is completing the insurance application and
paying the initial premium while the insurer’s consideration is a promise to
pay policy benefits if the conditions stated in the policy occur.
Lawful Purpose
A contract will NOT be valid if it was

Made for a purpose that is illegal

Against the public interest (đi ngược lại lợi ích cộng đồng)
An agreement between two parties to commit a criminal act (hành vi phạm
tội), such as an agreement to kill an individual in exchange for money, is
not legally enforceable. A contract can’t require a party to the contract to
break the law.

Contractual Capacity
Remember that the requirements for a valid contract are:


Contractual capacity (năng lực giao kết hợp đồng)
Now that we’ve described the first three requirements, let’s see how the
parties to an insurance contract satisfy the contractual capacity
requirement.
Contractual Capacity for Insurance Companies
An insurance company acquires the contractual capacity to issue an
insurance policy by being licensed or authorized to do business by the proper
regulatory authority. A company that is not licensed or authorized does not
have the legal capacity to make an insurance contract.

===== Trang 23 / 26 =====

Contractual Capacity for Individuals
The law generally presumes that individuals who apply for insurance have
the contractual capacity to enter into a contract. However, a lack of full
contractual capacity is assumed when those individuals

Are minors (chưa đủtuổi vịthành niên)

Lack mental capacity (Thiếu khảnăng nhận thức)
Minors
Minors are people who have not reached the legal age of adulthood—18 in
most jurisdictions. Minors typically do not have full contractual capacity.
If a minor is issued an insurance policy, the policy is voidable by the minor,
which means that the minor can later decide to disaffirm (hủy bỏ) the
policy, and the insurer will have to return the premiums the minor paid for
the policy.
Mental Capacity
People who lack mental capacity (for example, declared insane (mất trí) or
mentally incompetent (không đủnăng lực hành vi dân sự) by a court of law)
or whose mental capacity is impaired (for example, intoxicated (bịảnh

hưởng bởi chất kích thích như rượu hoặc ma túy) or mentally ill (bệnh tâm
thần)) typically do not have full contractual capacity.
A person without mental capacity cannot enter into a valid insurance
contract. Thus, any such contract would be void. A person with impaired
mental capacity is like a minor in that an impaired person who later
regains competency (khôi phục năng lự) may be able to avoid the insurance
contract and receive a return of premiums.
