Meet Stakeholder Needs
An insurer’s overall direction is determined by its executives, with
guidance from the board of directors. In this module, we explore how an
insurer is organized and managed to balance the needs of a diverse set
of stakeholder groups, and how an insurer manages the risk it must take
in order to earn a return.
In this module, you’ll learn about

How an insurer’s board and executives manage the company ethically
to meet the needs of various stakeholders

The ways an insurer can be organized for success

How an insurer manages the various risks it faces to earn an
appropriate return

Welcome to LOMA 291: Improving the Bottom Line—Insurance Company Operations.
My name is Grace Avon and I am CEO of Forthright Financial, a life insurance company.
Like anybody, I want to be successful in my job and make sure that my company is
successful as well. But how do I know if I am being successful? How do you measure a
company's success?
Should I aim to maximize revenues, or profit, or stock price? Should I be most worried
about our ratings from the ratings agencies, about satisfying government regulators, or
about paying a dividend—to shareholders or to policyowners? Should I be aiming to
create an amazing workplace full of happy employees, or a company that other
companies and individuals love to do business with?
The answer to all these questions is "Yes!" But which factors should be the top priorities?
That's a difficult question, and different people would probably give a different answer.
As you'll see, Forthright must weigh the interests of several different groups. In this
course, we'll start by examining how an insurance company decides what goals it will
pursue. Then we'll discuss the roles of all the different areas of the company in
achieving these goals.

Corporate Governance
Learning Objective
Explain the governance role of a company's board of directors, and describe the impact
of governance on conflicting priorities.
Each stakeholder group has an interest in how the insurer operates—the decisions it
makes and the actions it takes.
Sometimes stakeholder groups share the same company goals. But other times these
groups have different interests and, as a result, different goals. For example, suppose
Forthright has some capital—money, that is—and it needs to decide how to spend it.
Some options could be

Declare a stockholder dividend

Declare a policyowner dividend

Purchase another company

Expand into a new product line
Which would you want the company to do?
Your answer depends on whether you're a stockholder, policyowner, employee, or
financial professional. That's why the company needs to

Balance the interests of the different stakeholder groups and

Prioritize each group's interests, if necessary

Governance and Ethics
Learning Objective
Define ethics, and identify mechanisms companies employ to foster a culture of ethical
behavior.

===== Trang 21 / 28 =====

Boards of directors fulfill their governance function, in part, by establishing a culture of
ethical conduct. It's important for a company to show a true commitment to high
ethical standards and for all employees—especially those at the top—to embrace these
standards. If employees see their leaders display ethical behavior, they're more likely
to trust the company and follow suit.
And companies with strong ethical cultures usually have more motivated and
committed employees.
Corporate scandals can damage a company's reputation and cost millions in settlement
payments. So when it comes to ethics, the old saying about "an ounce of prevention is
worth a pound of cure" couldn't be more true.
Ethics and Ethical Conduct
Ethics (A system of accepted standards of conduct and moral judgment that
combines the elements of honesty, integrity, and fair treatment.) is a system of
accepted standards of conduct and moral judgment. It includes honesty, integrity, and
fair treatment.
For most companies, the basic ethical rules start with respecting stakeholders
(including employees), competing fairly and within the law, and being a responsible
corporate citizen
Here are some basic components of ethical corporate behavior:

Follow accepted legal and moral principles of right and wrong

Uphold promises to customers

Perform high-quality work in a timely fashion

Keep private information confidential
