Types of Qualified Retirement Plans
Qualified employer-sponsored retirement plans are generally divided into two types:

A defined benefit (DB) plan, often referred to as a pension plan, states the
amount of the periodic benefit each plan participant will receive at retirement. A
formula in the plan typically provides either a dollar amount or a percentage
amount according to years of service or other objective factors.

A defined contribution (DC) plan specifies the contributions that the plan
sponsor agrees to make to the plan. The benefit that a participant will receive is
not determined in advance of the participant’s retirement but depends on the
investment performance of the funds in the plan.

Disadvantages of DB Plans

DB plans are typically expensive to set up and administer—even for the largest
companies.

Plan costs are determined by actuarial assumptions relating to employer
contributions and investment earnings that are difficult to predict from year to
year. In years in which plan investments don’t perform as well as expected, the
employer has to contribute more to the plan than anticipated, which can hurt the
company’s financial results.

DB benefits aren’t portable; however, an employee will receive vested benefit
amounts from the employer upon retirement.

DB benefit calculations are difficult for employees to understand.

want to set up a DB plan, many have turned to defined contribution (DC) plans.


