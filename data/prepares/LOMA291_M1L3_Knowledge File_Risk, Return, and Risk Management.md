Risk-Return Tradeoff

===== Trang 8 / 46 =====

Whenever you find an opportunity for a return, you find risk. The
relationship between risk and return is called the risk-return tradeoff (The
relationship between risk and return that affects virtually all business
transactions. In general, all other factors being equal, the greater the risk
associated with an investment, the greater the expected return. The lower
the risk associated with an investment, the lower the expected return.).
In general, the riskier an investment, the greater the potential return on
the investment. Conversely, the less risky an investment, the lower the
expected return.

Earning an adequate return requires taking some amount of risk.

Earning a huge return requires taking a huge risk.

Where there's only a small risk, there's only a small potential return.

Where there's a large risk, there's a large potential return.

Elements of a Required Return
A return from any source can be expressed in terms of a rate of return
(The earnings on an investment expressed as a percentage of the
principal. )—a percentage of the amount invested (called the principal).

===== Trang 10 / 46 =====

Generally, an investor's required return (The return an investor needs to
earn in order to make the investment, given the risk of the investment;
typically found by adding the risk-free rate of return to the risk
premium. )—the rate of return the investor needs to earn in order to make
an investment—equals the sum of a risk-free rate (The return on a risk-
free investment—the least risky investment opportunity available. See
required return and risk premium.) and a risk premium (The component of
investment return that compensates the investor for taking on the risk
associated with a specific investment; the rate of return required in
addition to the risk-free rate for the investor to make the investment.).
Risk-Free Rate + Risk Premium = Required Return
An investor expects to earn some compensation even when an investment is
risk-free. The amount an investor expects to earn from a risk-free
investment is called the risk-free rate.

Interest-rate risk (The risk that unpredictable movements or stagnation in

The third line of defense, the internal audit, determines if the first two lines
of defense have been performing to expectations.
Internal audits systematically examine the company's risk management to
determine what is working and what could be improved.
Internal audits are different from the other two lines of defense in two
important ways:

Objectivity. An audit measures facts and evidence only. It doesn't
weigh risk against potential reward—it only reports results.

Organizational independence. An auditor reports only to the highest
level of management. This helps keep the audit unbiased.
The third line of defense gives us the facts we need to be sure
that risk management is working as intended.
