Nonforfeiture Provision
Sometimes the owner of a cash value policy decides he no longer wants or
needs insurance. What happens to any cash value?
The nonforfeiture provision provides options to such a policyowner when
coverage terminates through policy lapse or surrender.
Most policies have an automatic nonforfeiture benefit if the owner hasn’t
chosen an option when a policy lapses.

===== Trang 31 / 38 =====

Net Cash Surrender Value
The amount of cash value available to the policyowner when coverage
terminates is the net cash surrender value.
Insurers calculate the net cash surrender value by adjusting the cash value
as follows:
The cash value of any paid-up additions
Any premiums paid in advance
Any accumulated policy dividends
Minus any policy loans and policy loan interest outstanding

Automatic Premium Loan
Many cash value policies also include an automatic premium loan option.
The insurer will automatically pay an overdue premium by making a loan
against the policy’s cash value, as long as the cash value equals or exceeds
the amount of premium due.
Using this option keeps the original policy, including all supplemental
benefits, in force.

Life Insurance Policy Exclusions
Insurance policies exclude certain causes of death from policy coverage. The
most common exclusion is death by suicide for a specified period—usually
two years—from the date of policy issue.
The suicide exclusion provision governs the payment of a policy’s proceeds if
the insured dies by suicide.
If suicide occurs during the exclusion period, the insurer pays no death
benefit but does refund the premiums paid. If suicide occurs after the
exclusion period, the insurer pays the policy’s full death benefit.
