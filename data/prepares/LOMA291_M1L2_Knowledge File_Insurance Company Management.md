Organizing
Organizing (The management function of establishing authority,
responsibility, and accountability for accomplishing various company
activities, including assembling people and other company resources.) means
defining a structure for an organization to enable it to meet its goals.
Companies do this by setting up specialized organizational units to perform
work activities.
A distinct type of work is often called a function (A distinct type of work or
an aspect of operations or management that requires specialized knowledge
or technical skill.). So, when organized so that each unit performs a distinct
type of work, organizational units are often referred to as functional areas.

Managerial Roles
Learning Objective
Characterize the distinct roles of executives, middle managers, and
supervisors.
Now that we have introduced the functions of management, let's discuss the
different levels of management. A typical organization's management
consists of executives, middle managers, and supervisors.
Each level of management serves a unique purpose. Any manager at any
level will normally wear more than one hat and perform more than just
one function. Let's look at the differences in the jobs of these managers.
Executive Officers
The management domain of executives is sometimes called strategic
management (The top level of management.)
Executives (also known as officers, or senior-level managers) perform
strategic planning (Setting long-term corporate goals and the broad, overall
courses of action to achieve these goals. )—setting major long-term
corporate goals and broad approaches to achieve them.
A company's executives and officers are the chief executive officer, president,
other chief officers, vice presidents, and any other individuals appointed as
officers by the board of directors.
