===== Trang 23 / 56 =====

Remember controls? A budget can be used as a steering control, a concurrent control,
and a feedback control.

Steering Controls: Managers develop revenue and expense projections for a
specific period, usually one year. They use these projections to create a budget,
which provides financial goals and guidance for activities during the year.

Concurrent Controls: During the year, managers compare their actual results to
budgeted projections, and then alter their activities accordingly. For example, if
expenses are ahead of budget at mid-year, the company or a unit might cut
back on certain expenses for the rest of the year.

Feedback Controls: At year end, managers compare actual operating results to
the budgeted numbers. Deviations between the two sets of numbers guide future
activities and future budgets.
Budget Variances
At the end of the year, a difference between actual results and the budget is known as
a budget variance. Managers probe budget variances to find what caused them. When
they understand the causes, they can make adjustments for the future.
