Market Analysis
A market analysis is an evaluation of all factors that might affect product
sales. The market analysis attempts to demonstrate that market demand
exists to support a proposed product.
Product Design Objectives
Product design objectives are the product's basic characteristics, benefits,
fees and charges, any limitations on issuing policies, and operational and
administrative requirements such as underwriting guidelines. These
objectives guide technical product design later.
Feasibility Study
A feasibility study evaluates the operational and technical viability of
producing the product and the impact of the new product on the
company's existing products.
Marketing Plan
As described in the previous lesson, a marketing plan provides a detailed
description of the marketing goals for a product or product line, and
describes the strategies and the implementation and control efforts the
company intends to use to achieve those goals.
Marketing Projection
A marketing projection is an estimate of future sales, including sales volume
(number of units sold), sales revenue (dollar volume of sales), expenses, and
profits. Early estimates are rough but become more refined as the process
progresses.

Technical Product Design
Learning Objective
Explain the technical product design stage of product development.
After the risk committee or other executive group approves a proposal for a new
insurance product, the product development team moves on to technical product
design.
In this stage, the product development team uses the more general product proposal
as the basis for a highly detailed product design. Two primary elements included in the
technical product design are the product's financial structure and the contract forms
for the product.

===== Trang 31 / 47 =====

The technical product design phase typically involves a three-part process:
1. Establishing a financial design
2. Testing a financial design
3. Budgeting and scheduling
Establishing a Financial Design
The process of establishing a financial design is extremely technical. Actuaries—highly
trained experts in the mathematics of insurance—create the financial design of a new
insurance or annuity product by answering questions involving the product's

Premium rates

Account or transaction fees and charges

Surrender charge schedules

Cash value growth schedules or formulas

Underwriting standards, tables, and risk classes

Reinsurance strategy and retention limit

===== Trang 32 / 47 =====

The goals of financial design are

Ensuring that the company will be able to cover future benefits and expenses,
even in crisis conditions

Creating a package of benefits and costs that will have marketplace appeal for
purchasers and financial professionals

Providing an adequate return to the company’s owners
Testing a Financial Design
How do actuaries test proposed financial designs? Primarily by using financial models:
computer programs that describe the product mathematically. A model shows how the
financial values below interact over a long future period.
Financial models show

Cash inflows: premiums, deposits, charges, fees, investment income, etc.

Cash outflows: benefits, surrenders, expenses, etc.

Profitability
Models also "stress test" the product's financial design, which means testing product
performance in extremely unfavorable conditions.

Policy Filing

===== Trang 36 / 47 =====

U.S. regulations require insurers to file new product contract forms and any other
legally required documents with regulatory agencies in each state in which the insurer
will be offering the products. This process is called policy filing.
During policy filing, state insurance regulators review the contract form. Among other
things, they check that it includes all the necessary information identifying the insurer
offering the product. They make sure it includes all the policy provisions required in
that state and that it does not include any provisions prohibited in that state.
They also ensure that the policy satisfies current readability requirements. These
requirements ensure that the policy can be understood by people who are not legal
experts, and are concerned with sentence length, word length, and the amount of
technical and legal language included in the contract.

Information Systems
Bringing necessary systems on line is a critical Day 1 activity. The IT staff frequently
needs to make changes to the company's existing systems to support the new product.
Sometimes, they have to develop or purchase new systems.
With the systems changes, the operational areas frequently must modify the processes

documented, so the operational staff can be trained properly.
It can take months to get everything done, so systems activities need to be started as

Business Intelligence Systems
A business intelligence system analyzes data and information captured and processed
by transaction processing systems and produces reports to help management and
employees evaluate business situations, make decisions, and solve problems.
One form of business intelligence is business analytics, which includes a variety of
technologies, applications, and processes as well as statistical and quantitative analysis.
Business analytics provide detailed information such as product sales patterns to help
management make decisions or solve problems.
Expert Systems

===== Trang 42 / 47 =====

An expert system stores knowledge in the form of rules and applies those rules to




Claim administration

Detection of claim fraud

Product Launch
Finally, when the insurer has completed at least the Day 1 implementation activities,


IT and business process changes are put into operation

Advertising and sales support materials are distributed


But who do you think the insurer most wants to get excited about the product?
That's right: financial professionals. The insurer works hard to catch financial
professionals' attention, teach them about the new product, and generate enthusiasm
in order to encourage them to recommend the product to their clients.

If we think the product is the problem, we can redesign

If a product can't be fixed—that is, if its failure stems from factors outside our

Poor initial sales results don't always mean trouble. New product sales usually grow

When a product is taken off the market, the policies already sold can remain in force
for a long time. And unless we sell those policies to another insurer, we're obligated to
provide service and benefits to those customers.
Product Development Process Evaluation
The control cycle can be used for another purpose:
To identify strengths and weaknesses of the product development process.
Knowing what works—and what doesn't—helps:

Improve the success rate of future product development projects;

Reduce product development costs;

Improve new product performance; and

Shorten the time needed to bring new products to market.
