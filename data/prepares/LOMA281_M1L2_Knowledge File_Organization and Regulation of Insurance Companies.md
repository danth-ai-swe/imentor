In the 1990s, deregulation (việc nới lỏng quy định) allowed convergence (sự
hội tụ) which resulted in consolidation (sựhợp nhất) in the financial services
industry.
Consolidation has occurred primarily (chủyếu) through mergers (hợp nhất)
and acquisitions (mua lại).
Merger: Two companies are combined into one company. One company
survives as a legal entity and the other company ceases (chấm dứt) to exist.
Acquisition: One company purchases a controlling interest (phần lớn quyền
kiếm soát) in another company. After the acquisition, both companies
continue to exist as legal entities (thực thểpháp lý or tư cách pháp lý).
Acquisitions can include the purchase of an entire company or just one line
of business (một mảng kinh doanh cụthể).
The result of consolidation is that companies that previously sold only one
type of financial product now may offer multiple types of financial products.
Globalization
Another trend impacting financial institutions is globalization.
Financial services institutions, particularly those from Western Europe and
North America, have been expanding their customer bases in nations outside
their countries of origin.
Often companies have to purchase financial institutions in other countries to
gain entry into foreign markets.

The Insurance Industry
People and businesses purchase insurance products to cover three types of
risk:
Personal risk: Risks arising from the possibility of death (tửvong), illness
(bệnh tật), injury (chấn thương), inability to work (mất khảnăng lao động),
or outliving one’s savings (sống quá lâu khiến cạn kiệt tiền tiết kiệm)
Property damage risk: Risks occurring from damage to (hư hỏng),
destruction of (phá hủy), or the disappearance (mất mát) of real property
such as a home, an automobile (ô tô), or personal belongings (đồdùng cá
nhân)
Liability risk: Risks occurring from acts (hành vi) or omissions (thiếu sót)
that result in harm (gây thiệt hại) to others or their property
Life and health insurance companies insure personal risks.
Property/Casualty insurance companies (công ty bảo hiểm tài sản và thiệt
hại) insure property damage and liability risks.

Insurance Regulation
Learning Objective
Identify the two primary types of insurance regulation (quy định bảo hiểm)
in most countries.
Describe the roles of the U.S. state and federal governments with respect to
insurance regulation.
Regulation
The insurance industry plays a vital role in the stability of a nation’s
economy by protecting people against economic loss and by providing them
with mechanisms (cơ chế) for saving and investing.
Governments play a vital role in maintaining the public’s trust in the
insurance industry by regulating (quy định) and monitoring (giám sát) the
solvency (khảnăng thanh toán) and ethical conduct of insurance companies.
Two Goals of Insurance Regulation
1. To ensure that every insurance company remains financially sound—is
able to meet its debts and pay contractual obligations when they come due.
Solvency Regulation (Quy định vềkhảnăng thanh toán)
Solvency regulations focus on making sure that insurance companies remain
solvent. Solvency regulation is known as prudential regulation (quy định
thận trọng) in many countries.
Most countries impose minimum financial requirements (áp đặt các yêu cầu
tài chính) that an insurer must meet before it can conduct business.

===== Trang 14 / 25 =====

Laws also regulate (điều chỉnh) the financial aspects of an insurance policy’s
design and amounts of assets that insurers must maintain to support the
liabilities of the company to its policyowners.
If an insurer were to have financial difficulties, laws are also in place in
many countries and in all states in the United States to allow the
government to act to protect the public interest. (lợi ích công cộng)
2. To ensure that every insurance company conducts its business fairly with
honesty, integrity (đạo đức), and competence (năng lực).
Market Conduct Regulation (Quy định vềhành vi thịtrường)
Many countries have enacted market conduct laws (luật hành vi thịtrường)
that regulate (điều chỉnh) how insurance companies conduct business.
Market conduct laws help ensure that customers receive fair and accurate
information before they buy insurance and equitable treatment (đối xửcông
bằng) during the buying process.
Such laws typically regulate the form and content of insurance
advertisements to make sure they don’t mislead consumers as well as
various aspects of the sales process.
Different Countries, Different Regulatory Systems
Countries also may differ in their systems for enacting and overseeing
insurance companies.
Many countries centralize insurance regulation under the supervision of the
national government. For example, in India, the sole insurance regulator is
the national Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority (IRDA).

The McCarran-Ferguson Act (1945) is a federal law (luật liên bang) that
allows state governments (chính quyền tiểu bang) to regulate the business of
insurance as long as Congress considers the state legislation (luật pháp tiểu

state regulation to be inadequate (không đầy đủ).
The Dodd-Frank Act enacted in 2010 created a new federal agency, the
Federal Insurance Office (FIO), with authority to monitor the insurance
industry, identify areas with inadequate state regulation, and handle
international insurance issues.

More on State Insurance Departments
To ensure compliance with market conduct laws, state regulators look at
insurers’ nonfinancial operations. State regulators perform market conduct
examinations of companies’ operations to ensure compliance with laws.
Advertising materials are reviewed by state regulators. They also regulate
the policy forms that insurers may use to sell insurance products within the
state. An insurer must file a copy of each policy form it plans to use with
the state insurance department
To ensure compliance with solvency legislation, state regulators conduct
formal investigations of an insurer’s financial condition at least every three
to five years.
Regulators also require that insurers file reports each year illustrating that
the insurer has met all financial requirements imposed by state laws.

Other Federal Laws
Even federal laws that aren’t designed to regulate insurance directly affect
the insurance industry. Here are some examples.
Consumer Protection Laws Consumer protection laws prohibit insurers from
engaging in unfair competition or unfair or deceptive business practices,
primarily related to interstate advertising.
Privacy Laws Privacy laws affect how insurers collect and use personal
information about their customers.
Income Tax Laws Income tax laws encourage people to invest and employers
to provide retirement savings plans and greatly impact the design of
insurance products.
Antiterrorism Laws (Luật chống khủng bố) Antiterrorism laws require
insurers to take steps to detect and prevent illegal activities used to finance
terrorism
Corporate Governance Laws (Luật Quản trịDoanh nghiệp) Corporate
governance laws impose certain requirements (áp đặt các yêu cầu nhất định)
on corporations regarding organization, accountability, and disclosure. One
such law, the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, protects investors and other
stakeholders by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate
disclosures made in accordance with securities laws.

Guaranty Associations
In each state in the United States, an organization that protects
policyowners, insureds, beneficiaries, annuitants (người nhận niên kim),
payees, and assignees (người được chuyển nhượng) against losses that might
result from the impairment (sựsuy giảm năng lực tài chính) or insolvency
(mất thanh toán) of a life or health insurer that does business in the state.
Insurers are required to participate in a state’s guaranty association in
order to be licensed in that state.
A life and health insurance guaranty association is comprised (bảo gồm) of
life and health insurers operating in that state. The association’s goal is to
provide policyowners and beneficiaries with protection against the possibility
of insurers becoming insolvent.
If an insurance company operating in a state becomes insolvent, that state’s
guaranty association covers the company’s benefit payments up to state-
mandated maximums. Most states guarantee payment of up to $300,000
in benefits.
